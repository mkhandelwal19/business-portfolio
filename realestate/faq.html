Property questions, straight answers.

The things buyers and sellers most often need to understand before they begin.

A broker sources, filters, and presents properties relevant to your requirements; negotiates price on your behalf; verifies legal status; and coordinates the documentation and registration process. Saha Properties charges 1% of the transaction value — from both buyer and seller separately. There are no hidden charges. We do not receive developer commissions that would influence what we show you. If we are working with you as a buyer, our incentive is aligned entirely with getting you the right property at the right price.

Title verification involves reviewing the chain of ownership documents (sale deeds) going back at least 30 years, checking for encumbrances at the sub-registrar's office, verifying mutation in the local authority's land records, and confirming there are no pending dues on the property. For properties in Kolkata, this process should always be done by a lawyer familiar with West Bengal property law. We work with two trusted lawyers and coordinate this process for all buyers as part of our service.

RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) registration is mandatory for all new residential projects with more than 8 units or 500 sq m of land. RERA registration means the developer has disclosed the project plan, approvals, delivery timeline, and financial details. It also means buyers have a legal avenue for complaints if delivery is delayed or quality doesn't match. We only list RERA-registered new projects. For resale properties, RERA doesn't apply but legal verification is even more important.

Budget an additional 8–12% of the property value for transaction costs. Stamp duty in West Bengal: 6% (men), 5% (women buyers get 1% concession). Registration fee: 1%. GST on new properties under construction: 5% (1% for affordable housing). Broker fee: 1%. Lawyer fee: ₹15,000–₹40,000. Loan processing fee: 0.5–1% of loan amount. Society transfer charges vary. We prepare a transaction cost breakdown for every deal so there are no surprises.

We compare the asking price against recent registrations in the same building and locality (publicly available from the sub-registrar's office), the current circle rate set by the government, the cost-per-sqft benchmarks for that area, and recent comparable sales we have personally handled. We share this analysis with every buyer. If a property is overpriced, we tell you. If it is priced below market (and there's a reason why), we investigate that too.

Banks lend on resale properties as readily as new ones, provided the property has clear title and the age of the building is within the loan tenure you are applying for (most banks won't lend on buildings older than 40 years if the loan tenure takes the total building age past 60). We coordinate with SBI, HDFC, ICICI, and LIC Housing Finance for home loans and routinely negotiate rates 0.1–0.25% below standard walk-in offers. The loan can typically be processed in 2–3 weeks for a straightforward application.

January–March tends to have the best inventory and is when many developers push end-of-financial-year pricing. October–November (post-Durga Puja) sees fresh listings from families who waited for an auspicious period to sell. The worst time to negotiate is December, when sellers hold out for the New Year. This is general guidance — the right time to buy is when the right property is available at the right price, not when the season is convenient.

Currently (2026), New Town (Rajarhat) continues to see the highest appreciation for residential properties. Sector V and its surroundings remain the strongest commercial micro-market for IT-adjacent office space. For rental yield, Lake Gardens and Ballygunge offer 3–4% annual yield — not extraordinary, but with steady appreciation. Areas around Joka (IIM campus) have seen speculative interest. We advise against buying purely for appreciation in areas where infrastructure promises have been pending for more than 5 years.

You can execute a Special Power of Attorney (SPA) in favour of a trusted person in Kolkata for registration and documentation purposes. The SPA must be notarised and registered. Alternatively, you can be present in person for the final registration — which takes one day. Many of our outstation clients fly in specifically for registration day and we coordinate the entire process so everything is ready and it's completed in a single visit. We strongly advise against general or unlimited PoAs for property transactions.
