Our Story

Started with a single showcase.

In 1962, Ramkrishna Mallick opened a small jewellery shop at 14 Bow Bazaar Street with one glass showcase and tools passed down from his father. He made everything by hand — no machines, no shortcuts — and sold to the families of Bow Bazaar who trusted him for generations.

His son Shyamal expanded the workshop in the 1980s, training six craftsmen from the neighbourhood and introducing diamond jewellery alongside the gold. When Sudipta — third generation — took over in 2002, she introduced BIS hallmarking across the entire inventory, before it became mandatory, because she believed customers deserved certainty about what they were buying.

Today, Arjun Mallick — the fourth generation — runs the store with his mother. The showcase is now a showroom. But the craftsmen still work in the same workshop behind the store, using the same techniques Ramkrishna taught.

What We Stand For

Six principles, six decades.

BIS Hallmarked

Every gold piece carries a BIS hallmark — not because we have to, but because we started before it was required. Purity is non-negotiable.

IGI Certified Diamonds

Every diamond sold carries an IGI certificate specifying cut, colour, clarity, and carat. We do not sell uncertified stones.

Handcrafted

Custom pieces are made by hand in the Bow Bazaar workshop. No mass production. The craftsman who designs your piece makes it.

Fair Exchange

Old gold exchanged at current market rate, always. No deductions for "making charges lost." We treat exchange with the same honesty as a fresh purchase.

Lifetime Cleaning

Every piece purchased from Mallika Jewels is eligible for lifetime free cleaning and polishing at the store. Bring it in any time.

Family Relationships

Most of our customers are families who have been with us for two or three generations. We remember names, weddings, and anniversaries because that's what family businesses do.
