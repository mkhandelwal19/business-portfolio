FAQ

Your questions, answered.

Everything you need to know before you visit or commission a piece.

BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) hallmarking certifies the purity of gold. A 22K piece stamped 916 means it contains 91.6% pure gold. A 18K piece stamped 750 means 75%. Without a hallmark, you cannot verify purity independently. All gold sold at Mallika Jewels is BIS hallmarked — 22K pieces for traditional jewellery, 18K for diamond-set pieces where the harder alloy is required. We have been hallmarking since before it became mandatory because we believe customers deserve certainty.

Bring your old gold to the store. We test purity in front of you using a certified acid test. We offer current market rate for the tested purity — no deductions for making charges, design, or age of the piece. The exchange value is applied against any purchase in our store. We do not buy gold outright for cash; exchange is against new purchases only.

3 to 6 weeks from the date design is finalised and a 50% advance is paid. Simple designs (plain gold rings, pendants, chains) take 3 weeks. Complex pieces with Kundan or Polki work, or large bridal sets, take 5–6 weeks. We share progress photos during crafting. Bridal orders should be placed at least 8–10 weeks before the wedding to allow for fittings and adjustments.

An IGI (International Gemological Institute) certificate describes a diamond's 4Cs — cut, clarity, colour, and carat weight. It is an objective grading report, not a valuation. The certificate stays with the stone for its lifetime. We provide IGI certificates for all diamonds above 0.20 carats. Smaller accent stones are described by total carat weight and average quality. The certificate does not guarantee resale price, which depends on market conditions at the time of sale.
