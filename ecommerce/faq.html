FAQ

Common questions, honest answers.

About products, shipping, returns, and how we work with artisans.

Every product ships with an artisan card — the name, village, and craft tradition of the person who made it. For GI-tagged crafts (Jamdani, Tant, Shitalpati), we include the certification documentation. We visit our artisan partners at least twice a year and document the process for our blog. If you ever receive something that looks machine-made, send us a photo and we will investigate and refund without question.

Yes. Artistic variation is the signature of handmade craft. A Tant saree woven on a pit loom will have slight variations in the weave density. A Dhokra figurine cast using the lost-wax process will have unique surface texture. Kantha stitching will vary in density. These are not defects — they are proof of handcraft. If variation is significant enough that the piece looks different from the listing photo, we will replace it. But minor variation is part of what you are buying.

In-stock items: 3–5 business days across India. Made-to-order items (noted on product page): 2–4 weeks from order date. We ship from Kolkata using BlueDart or Delhivery depending on the destination. All orders above ₹999 ship free. Fragile items (terracotta, pottery) are packed with extra protection and shipped as fragile — occasionally this adds 1 day to delivery.

We accept returns within 15 days for two reasons: the item arrived damaged, or it differs significantly from the product description. We do not accept returns for colour variation (screen colours are approximate) or artistic variation (see above). For damaged items, email or WhatsApp us a photo and we'll arrange pickup and replacement or refund within 5 business days. Custom / made-to-order items are not returnable unless damaged.

For some crafts, yes. Tant sarees can be ordered in custom colour combinations with a 4-week lead time. Kantha pieces can be made to custom sizes. Dhokra figurines are not customisable — the design is specific to each artisan family's tradition. Patachitra scrolls can be commissioned with specific subject matter. Contact us before ordering if you want custom specifications.

All handloom sarees come with a care label. General guidance: first wash in cold water with mild detergent, alone (colours may bleed initially). Do not wring — lay flat to dry in shade. Iron on medium heat, fabric side. Do not dry-clean silk Tant — hand-wash only. Store folded in a cotton cloth bag, not a plastic cover. For Jamdani and Muslin, store separately as delicate weaves can snag on heavier fabrics.

On average, 55–65% of what you pay reaches the artisan family directly. This includes the cost of materials, which the artisan often sources themselves. Our margin covers platform operations, packaging, shipping coordination, quality checking, and our team. We publish this breakdown on every product page. The industry average for craft marketplaces is 10–20% to the artisan — we have published a comparison on our blog.
