Why Tant is the most democratic cloth Bengal ever made
Tant was never a luxury. It was everyday wear — affordable, breathable, and woven in large quantities by cooperatives in Nadia and Hooghly. Its return as a fashionable choice is interesting: the same cloth the upper middle class once replaced with synthetic fabric is now sold at a premium. What that says about taste, status, and craft.

Craft Story
Jamdani: why the most ancient Bengali cloth is also the most fragile
Jamdani Muslin is woven on a pit loom without a shuttle — each supplementary thread is inserted by hand. A 6-yard saree takes two weavers 4–6 weeks to complete. The cloth is so fine it has historically been called "woven air." A GI tag protects its name; nothing protects the number of people who know how to make it.

Natural dyes will bleed in the first wash. Chemical dyes will not. Natural dyes fade differently over time — becoming richer, more nuanced. Chemical dyes fade flat.
