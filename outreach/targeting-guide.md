## Where to Find Target Businesses (Free)

### Tier 1 — Highest Intent (They're Already Looking)

1. **JustDial** — justdial.com/Kolkata  
   Filter by category → look for listings with *no website URL* or a broken link → their phone/address is public. Search: "restaurant Kolkata no website" or browse by neighbourhood.

2. **IndiaMART Kolkata Directory** — dir.indiamart.com/kolkata  
   B2B businesses actively using directories = high intent. Many have Gmail addresses listed.

3. **Sulekha** — sulekha.com/[category]/kolkata  
   Service businesses (salons, yoga, clinics) list here when they have no website. Emails often on profile pages.

4. **Google Maps** — Search "[category] [neighbourhood] Kolkata" → click listings → look for "No website" tag or a bare Facebook link.

5. **Facebook Business Pages** — Search "[category] Kolkata" → filter Pages → find pages with <500 followers, no website linked, recent posts = active but growing.

### Tier 2 — Discovery (You Find Them First)

6. **Instagram** — Search hashtags: `#kolkatarestaurant`, `#kolkatafashion`, `#kolkatacafe`, `#kolkatajewellery`, `#kolkatafitness`, `#kolkataclasses`, `#kolkataboutique`  
   Look for: Active posts (3+ per week), under 2,000 followers, link-in-bio = nothing or linktree, based in Kolkata.

7. **Swiggy / Zomato** — Restaurants listed there with no website URL in their description. These owners are digitally active (they integrated Swiggy) but haven't built a standalone brand site.

8. **Local newspapers / supplements** — Telegraph Kolkata, Anandabazar Patrika weekend supplements often feature new business openings with owner names. Google the owner name + business name to find contact.

9. **MagicBricks / 99acres** — Real estate agents and small builders list here without independent websites. Their phone numbers are public; many use Gmail.

10. **Trade Associations** — CCIА (Chamber of Commerce & Industry, WB), Bengal Retailers Association, HRAWI (hospitality) — publish member directories.

## Target Business Profiles by Category

### Restaurants & Cafés
**Who to target:** Neighbourhood restaurants 2–5 years old, 50–200 Zomato reviews, Instagram active but no website, no online ordering link.  
**Where they are:** Behala, Tollygunge, Jodhpur Park, Salt Lake, New Town, Gariahat, Shyambazar.  
**Pain point:** "Zomato takes 25% commission. A website with direct ordering saves money."  
**Hook:** Show them the Nizami Biryani or Aangan Thali case study.

### Boutiques & Fashion
**Who to target:** Handloom saree shops, kurta boutiques, ethnic wear designers — active on Instagram with great photos but no shopping site.  
**Where they are:** Gariahat market, Lake Market, New Market, Hatibagan, New Town.  
**Pain point:** Instagram DMs get lost; no way to take orders while sleeping.  
**Hook:** Show Studio Indu case study. Emphasise "your Instagram followers can now shop directly."

### Clinics & Healthcare
**Who to target:** GP clinics, physiotherapy centres, dental clinics, diagnostic labs — opened 2019–2024, no appointment booking system.  
**Where they are:** Behala, Garia, Dum Dum, Alipore, Ballygunge.  
**Pain point:** Patients call at odd hours; front desk overwhelmed. Website = 24/7 appointment booking.  
**Hook:** Show Basu Clinic case study.

### Salons & Spas
**Who to target:** Neighbourhood salons charging ₹500–₹2,000 per service, have loyal clientele, never had a website.  
**Where they are:** Every neighbourhood — focus on upscale areas: Alipore, Ballygunge, Salt Lake Sector V, New Town.  
**Pain point:** No way for customers to browse services and prices before walking in.  
**Hook:** "Your Google Business page alone doubles the calls you get."

### Jewellery
**Who to target:** Family-owned jewellers, silver/imitation jewellery exporters on IndiaMart, bridal jewellery boutiques.  
**Where they are:** Burrabazar, Shyambazar, Hatibagan, or boutique areas like Park Street.  
**Pain point:** Brides from Delhi/Mumbai can't browse the collection before visiting. "Show brides your full collection before they book their flight."  
**Hook:** Pearl Jewels case study.

### Real Estate
**Who to target:** Independent brokers, small builders with 1–3 active projects, co-working space owners.  
**Where they are:** New Town, Rajarhat, Garia, Tollygunge.  
**Pain point:** MagicBricks / 99acres = lost in a sea of listings. Their own site = direct inquiries.  
**Hook:** Show real estate template. Emphasise "your own brand, not buried on a platform."

### Yoga & Wellness
**Who to target:** Yoga teachers who went independent post-COVID, Ayurveda practitioners, meditation centres.  
**Where they are:** South Kolkata (Ballygunge, Jodhpur Park, Dhakuria), Salt Lake.  
**Pain point:** No way to take class bookings or sell memberships online.  
**Hook:** Ananta Yoga case study.

### Sweet Shops & Bakeries
**Who to target:** Traditional mishti doi / rosogolla shops expanding to home delivery, fusion bakeries.  
**Where they are:** Bhowanipur, North Kolkata (Shyambazar, Sinthee), Hatibagan.  
**Pain point:** Festival season orders are chaos on WhatsApp.  
**Hook:** Mondal Mishti case study. "Take Durga Puja pre-orders on your site — no WhatsApp madness."

### Coaching & Tuition
**Who to target:** IIT/NEET coaching centres, school tuition teachers going independent, language institutes.  
**Where they are:** Jadavpur, Garia, Salt Lake, Ultadanga (for competitive exams).  
**Pain point:** Word-of-mouth only. No way to show results, testimonials, or collect enquiries while offline.  
**Hook:** "Parents searching 'best coaching Garia' will find you first."

## How to Find Owner Email Addresses (Step-by-Step)

1. **Google Maps** → Open business listing → "Website" column often shows email in footer.
2. **Instagram bio** → Many owners put Gmail in bio directly.
3. **JustDial profile** → Phone visible always; email sometimes behind login.
4. **IndiaMART** → Free membership required to see email; worth creating an account.
5. **Facebook Page → About tab** → Often has business email.
6. **Hunter.io** → If you have their domain (even a Wix/Squarespace site), Hunter finds emails.
7. **LinkedIn** → Search "[Business Name] Kolkata" → message on LinkedIn first if no email found.
8. **In person** → Walk into the shop, ask for a visiting card. 100% hit rate. Do this for high-value targets (jewellers, real estate).
